Chelsea predicted lineup

Chelsea should go with a mobile front line and a midfield built around Moises Caicedo and Enzo Fernandez, hoping to match City’s technical level for stretches and attack quickly when space opens.

Manchester City predicted lineup

Manchester City are likely to control more of the ball, with their usual patient buildup and enough attacking quality between the lines to force Chelsea into long defensive phases.

Predicted Manchester City starting XI: Ederson; Kyle Walker, Ruben Dias, Josko Gvardiol, Nathan Ake; Rodri, Kevin De Bruyne, Bernardo Silva; Phil Foden, Erling Haaland, Jeremy Doku.

Team news

Chelsea’s key team-news question is whether they can field enough defensive balance behind their attacking players. Against City, one weak link in transition coverage can decide the match quickly.

City have more room to absorb changes because their system remains stable. The most important part is whether the midfield core and main attacking threats are available together.

Why this game looks this way

Chelsea can create moments if they break City’s pressure and run into space, but over 90 minutes City still have the more reliable possession game and the cleaner attacking structure.

That makes this less about whether Chelsea can compete for stretches and more about whether they can survive long enough without losing control in the middle of the pitch.

Score prediction

Chelsea can score, especially at home, but City should still create the better overall chances and control enough of the match to edge the result.
