Bayern Munich predicted lineup

Bayern are likely to lean into their usual front-foot pressure, with Musiala driving central progression and Kane acting as the reference point for both buildup and final-third finishing. The big question is not whether Bayern can create chances at home, but whether they can sustain that aggression without losing control behind the ball.

Predicted Bayern Munich starting XI: Manuel Neuer; Joshua Kimmich, Dayot Upamecano, Kim Min-jae, Alphonso Davies; Leon Goretzka, Konrad Laimer; Leroy Sane, Jamal Musiala, Kingsley Coman; Harry Kane.

Real Madrid predicted lineup

Real Madrid should look far less rushed. Their likely plan is to absorb Bayern’s pressure in phases, trust their midfield calm and attack the spaces that open when Bayern’s full-backs and central line step too high. That is why this tie always carries danger for the home side even when Bayern look on top territorially.

Predicted Real Madrid starting XI: Andriy Lunin; Dani Carvajal, Antonio Rudiger, Eder Militao, Ferland Mendy; Federico Valverde, Aurelien Tchouameni, Eduardo Camavinga, Jude Bellingham; Rodrygo, Kylian Mbappe.

Team news

The most important pre-match variables are in the defensive zones. Bayern need clarity over the goalkeeper and back-line stability, because even one missing defensive leader changes how safely they can press and how well they can recover against elite transition attacks.

For Real Madrid, the selection tension is more about control than volume. Their midfield and defensive screen matter more than adding another attacking body, because this tie is likely to be decided by who manages central spaces better in the second and third phases.

Why this game looks this way

This is the kind of Champions League matchup where raw territorial dominance can mislead. Bayern at home should push the game, but that does not automatically mean they control the tie. Against Real Madrid, too much verticality can become a trap if the press is broken cleanly.

That is why the key debate is not simply “Can Bayern win at home?” but “Can Bayern push for qualification without opening the exact spaces Real Madrid want?” If the answer is no, Bayern’s home advantage can quickly turn into an unstable game state.

Score prediction

Bayern should produce enough volume to score, especially with Kane and Musiala driving the central threat, but Real Madrid are built for this kind of knockout scenario. The stronger read is a high-level, tense game where Bayern’s pressure lands, but Madrid still find enough moments to keep the tie balanced.

Predicted score: Bayern Munich 2-2 Real Madrid
